Insertion Point

The option insertion point allows to place the center-line of the LINE Element in a different location. The options are shown in Figure below.

This generates changes in the section stiffness matrix, and they are considered using the following transformation matrix

\[ \mathbf{T}_\textrm{S} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & x_3 & x_2 \\ 0 & 1 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\,. \]

Thus, the resultant section stiffness matrix is obtained as

\[ \mathbf{K}_\textrm{S} = \mathbf{T}_\textrm{S}^\top \mathbf{K}_\textrm{c} \mathbf{T}_\textrm{S} \,, \]

where \(\mathbf{K}_\textrm{c}\) is the section stiffness matrix from the center-line, i.e., ip=10, and \(\mathbf{K}_\textrm{S}\) is the modified section stiffness matrix at the insertion point, and \(x_3, x_2\) are the coordinates with respect to ip=10 of the 3-axis and 2-axis.

Attention
The default option is ip=10, this is at the section geometric center.
If the Section is irregular (as the one in the figure), a rectangle of dimension \(H\) and \(B\) is used to define the insertion point options.
