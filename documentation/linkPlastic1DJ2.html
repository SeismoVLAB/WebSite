Plastic1DJ2

The Plastic1DJ2 keywords creates a uniaxial material class described in [Simo1998] with the constitutive equation given as

\[ \sigma = E \left( \epsilon - \epsilon^p \right) \nonumber \]

where \(\sigma\) is the uniaxial stress, \(\epsilon\) is the uniaxial strain, and \(E\) is the elasticity modulus. Figure shows the constitutive law of this material.

A Plastic1DJ2 material is created using the built-in parse-structure provided in the Parser.hpp,

MATERIAL Tag PLASTIC1DJ2 E nu rho K H sigmaY

Variable Description
Tag Unique material object identifier.
E Represents the elasticity modulus.
nu Represents the Poisson's ratio.
rho Represents the material density.
K Represents the kinematic modulus.
H Represents the hardening modulus.
sigmaYRepresents the yielding stress.
Attention
This material can only be assigned to one-dimensional elements such as: lin2DTruss2, kin2DTruss2, lin3DTruss2, kin3DTruss2 and ZeroLength1D elements.

Example

A uniaxial plastic-J2 material with elasticity modulus 200, Poisson's ratio 0.25, no density, no hardening modulus, and kinematic modulus 3.75 and yield stress of 0.25 is defined as:
MATERIAL 1 PLASTIC1DJ2 200.00 0.25 0.0 0.0 3.75 0.250
